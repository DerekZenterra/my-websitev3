"We Wouldn't Buy With Anyone Else" — Life at King + Crescent Two Years In

David and Lisa moved from a rental into their first Zenterra home in South Surrey. Two years later, the thing they keep coming back to isn't the quartz countertops or the 9-foot ceilings — it's the people.

March 12, 2026 6 min read King + Crescent
✍
Zenterra Team
Zenterra Developments
Share f 𝕏 in 🔗
David and Lisa at King + Crescent — portrait photography

David and Lisa were not looking for a new home when they first walked into the King + Crescent sales centre on 146A Street in South Surrey. They'd come out of curiosity — they'd driven past the site a dozen times and wondered what was going up. That was two and a half years ago. They moved in fourteen months later. Today, standing in their third-floor kitchen on a Tuesday afternoon, David laughs about how fast it all happened.

"We weren't even really in the market," he says, leaning against the island. "We had a rental. We thought we'd buy eventually. And then we walked in and met the team, and something just clicked."

The Sales Process

What struck them first, they say, wasn't the renders or the floor plans — it was the people in the room. The sales team didn't rush them, didn't push them toward the most expensive suite, and didn't pretend the building was finished when it wasn't. They showed them everything: the construction timeline, the features list, what was included and what wasn't.

"They never once made us feel like a transaction. They actually wanted us to find the right home — even if that wasn't theirs."

— Lisa, homeowner at King + Crescent

They ended up choosing a two-bedroom suite on the third floor. Not the largest. Not the flashiest. The one that felt like home. Within a few weeks they were registered, floor plan in hand, and tracking construction updates through the Zenterra homeowner portal.

/div>
/div>

David and Lisa's suite — Ash colour scheme, third floor, King + Crescent, South Surrey

The Orientation Walkthrough

Possession day, Lisa remembers, was the moment the relationship shifted. Their Customer Care Representative — the same person they'd been dealing with since registration — spent three hours walking them through every system in the suite. The HVAC. The hot water tank. The appliances. The shut-offs. Every question answered without impatience.

"He knew our suite better than we did," David says. "He'd obviously done this many times, but it never felt like a script. He genuinely cared that we understood our own home."

Two Years Later

The suite has been, in their words, "basically perfect." There were two small warranty items in the first year — a minor caulking issue around the shower and a thermostat calibration — both resolved within a week through the homeowner portal. Neither felt like a fight. Neither required a follow-up call.

When asked whether they'd buy with Zenterra again, David doesn't hesitate. "We're already looking at what's coming next," he says. "Clayton Crest, maybe. We're on the list."
